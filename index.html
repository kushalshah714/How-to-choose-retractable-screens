Common misconceptions

Things people often
get wrong.

If you have been comparing Wizard screens to what you have seen at hardware stores, here is what you need to know.

These are not DIY screens

Retractable screens must be measured on-site and professionally installed. A poor installation is the number one reason screens fail early. Off-the-shelf screens and custom retractable screens are completely different products.

Not all openings use the same screen

A standard door screen will not work on a 12-foot glass wall. Large openings need systems specifically engineered for their span. Using the wrong system means poor performance and early failure.

They still let air through

A common concern is that screens block airflow. They do not. The mesh allows fresh air to flow freely. Even shade-style meshes that reduce heat and glare still allow a comfortable breeze through your space.

Professional installation improves longevity

Correct alignment, tension, and fitting are what make a retractable screen glide smoothly for years. A certified Wizard dealer ensures your screen performs from day one and continues to for a long time after.

Motorized does not mean complicated

Motorized screens are controlled by a simple remote, a wall switch, or your existing smart home system. You do not need a smart home to use them. Most homeowners find them far easier than manual screens on large patios.

Cost is not comparable to hardware store screens

Comparing a Wizard screen to a roll of screen mesh from a hardware store is like comparing a custom kitchen to a flat-pack unit. They serve the same broad purpose but are entirely different products at different levels of quality and performance.
